## Key Discourses:
- The Three Metamorphoses: "The spirit becomes a camel; the camel, a lion; the lion, a child."
- The Despisers of the Body: "I am body entirely, and nothing else; and soul is only a word for something about the body."
- Reading and Writing: "He who writes in blood and aphorisms does not want to be read, but to be learnt by heart."
- The Tree on the Hill: "But it is the same with man as with the tree. The more he seeks to rise into the height and light, the more vigorously do his roots struggle earthward, downward, into the dark, the deep—into evil."
- Chastity: "It is not the anger of the enemy that you fear, but the love of the friend."
- The Friend: "Are you a slave? If so, you cannot be a friend. Are you a tyrant? If so, you cannot have friends."
- The Thousand and One Goals: "No people could live without first valuing; if a people will maintain itself, however, it must not value as its neighbour values."
- Neighbour-Love: "I do not teach you the neighbour, but the friend."
- Old and Young Women: "Everything in woman is a riddle, and everything in woman has one solution—its name is pregnancy."
- Child and Marriage: "You are young and wish for a child and marriage. But I ask you: Are you a man entitled to wish for a child?"
- Voluntary Death: "Many die too late, and some die too early."
- The Bestowing Virtue: "Gold, uncommonly, the highest virtue. Like gold, the bestowing virtue is uncommon."
- Self-Surpassing: "Whatever I create and however much I love it—soon I must oppose it and my love."
- The Dance-Song: "Only in the dance do I know how to speak the parable of the highest things."
- Redemption: "To redeem those that are the past, and to transform every 'It was' into 'Thus would I have it!'—that alone do I call redemption!"
- The Seven Seals: "If ever I have spread out a tranquil heaven above me, and flown with my own wings into my own heaven."
- The Drunken Song: "All joy wants eternity—wants deep, deep eternity!"
- The Sign: "This is my morning, my day begins: rise up now, rise up, great noon!"
